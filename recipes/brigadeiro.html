Brigadeiro - a little piece of heaven

Description

If you have never eaten brigadeiros before, you're in Bruno Mars' shoes: locked out of heaven. This brazilian classic
dessert is a mandatory presence in every birthday party in Brazil (I am not exagerating here), specially the ones made
for children. If you don't want to see children crying (or maybe killing people before becoming of age), better have
(a lot of) brigadeiros in their birthday parties.

If you aren't a big fan of sweets, like me, you'll still love it. But, if you're a chocolate lover... Get ready for the
best experience of your life (again, not exagerating).

But the true beauty of it lies in the simplicity: most of our awesome recipes need an ingredient characteristic of Brazil
(like tapioca flour), or something which probably was brought here from the portuguese or african people way back then.
But brigadeiro is different: it only needs four Ingredients for the traditional shape (little heaven spheres covered in
chocolate sprinkles), or three if you just want the version to be eaten with spoons.

Ingredients

  • Condensed milk
  • Non-salted butter (insert Julia Childs meme here)
  • Chocolate powder (if you put it in your milk to give it a chocolate flavor, works here)
  • Chocolate sprinkles

Steps

  1. Put the condensed milk, butter and chocolate powder in a pan
  2. Bring it to medium heat, stiring it
  3. Don't stop stiring until the mix texture starts to change
  4. When the texture has changed, pour it into a bowl or a plate and let it cool down

    5. After cooling down, you have what we call as a "spoon brigadeiro", because in this state we eat it with a spoon.
    If you want the party version which looks like a truffle (see image on top of page), follow these steps:

  5. Grease your hands in butter
  6. Take a portion of brigadeiro (1 spoon scoop) and put in your hand
  7. Shape it with your hands to make a sphere
  8. Cover its entire surface with chocolate sprinkles
  9. Wash your hands, eat it and discover what heaven tastes like
